Understanding Each Calculator
Know which metric to focus on depending on your current fitness stage.
💧
Body Fat Percentage
Measures the proportion of fat mass to total body weight. A far more meaningful health marker than BMI alone — you can be a "healthy" BMI but have dangerously high fat levels (skinny fat).
Body Composition US Navy Method
🔥
Daily Calorie Needs
Your TDEE — Total Daily Energy Expenditure — is the exact number of calories you burn in a day. Set calories below this to lose fat, above it to gain muscle. The foundation of any body transformation.
Fat Loss Muscle Gain Macros
🧬
Basal Metabolic Rate
The minimum calories your body needs to sustain life at complete rest — breathing, circulation, cell repair. Never eat below your BMR long-term; it triggers muscle loss and metabolic adaptation.
Metabolism Energy Balance
Body Fat % — US Navy Method
Male:
495 ÷ (1.0324 − 0.19077 × log(waist−neck) + 0.15456 × log(height)) − 450

Female:
495 ÷ (1.29579 − 0.35004 × log(waist+hip−neck) + 0.22100 × log(height)) − 450
Daily Calories — Mifflin-St Jeor + Activity
BMR (male): (10 × kg) + (6.25 × cm) − (5 × age) + 5
BMR (female): (10 × kg) + (6.25 × cm) − (5 × age) − 161

TDEE: BMR × Activity Factor
Goal: TDEE ± Calorie Adjustment
BMR — Three Formula Variants
Mifflin-St Jeor (default): as above

Harris-Benedict (revised):
Male: 88.362 + (13.397×kg) + (4.799×cm) − (5.677×age)

Katch-McArdle (lean mass):
370 + (21.6 × lean body mass in kg)
